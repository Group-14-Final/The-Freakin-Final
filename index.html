Fully Responsive Design

In this day and age, there is still a problem with minority groups not receiving equal opportunities for those who are labeled “privileged.” These disparities are seen through politics, job opportunities, healthcare, the criminal justice system, and education. All these topics are extremely crucial in making a change to our society. In education, it is extremely important that everyone receives the same education and is not missing out on basic, fundamental learning topics. Not only that, not incorporating cultures of those minority groups is a huge issue when looking at trying to make education equal for everyone. It is unethical to ignore those basics of those with different cultures, religions, and ethnicities.

The online article, “Emerging Issues in Indigenous Rights: Transformative Effects of the Recognition of Indigenous Peoples,” by Irène Bellier and Martinthere Préaud, provide a plethora of examples where indigenous people do not receive the same opportunities to learn some of the basic education that most people of the United States learn. At one point in the article, the authors state, “...education has historically been for states the privileged means to shape national communities” (Bellier and Préaud). Having one specific education that everyone must learn and follow blocks out the basics such as other languages, learning about specific histories, and learning about specific cultures. There should be a combination of education for all minorities and essentially anyone in school. This could be a pivotal change in education as minority groups can continue to learn about their culture while also learning what some see as the “basic education in the United States” and give the opportunity to those who are not in minority groups to learn about other cultures. Shaping national communities that have diversity is important and beneficial as it can create different solutions to problems, there can be incorporated information from different cultures. Being able to offer indigenous peoples education that those typically receive while also incorporating important aspects from their own ethnicity is a change that the education system needs to make. Another important quote that the authors wrote in the article is, “Achieving indigenous peoples' control over their education not only requires a transformation of their relationship to the state but also of educational institutions themselves” (Bellier and Préaud). With this being said, it is not only the relationship with the state that needs to be changed to have education changed, but also with the schools. If the schools are not willing to make changes to the education system with the criteria taught, then there will not be a change to make sure that everyone receives the same education while also incorporating aspects of different cultures, especially the culture of the indigenous peoples. Incorporating these changes will ultimately lead to a better education for most students, not just the indigenous peoples’ education, and can lead to new information being learned and a better understanding of the real world around those students. These changes would be crucial to each student in the United States and better the educational opportunities for students. Education opportunities for indigenous peoples and other minorities would significantly increase if there was a change to what students were taught to include every minority group possible.
